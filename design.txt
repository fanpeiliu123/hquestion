HQ (Haiwen,“海问”) AI Consultation Platform - Project Brief
Team: Group 13
Module Code: ENT208TC
1.Problem Statement: 
Critical Gap in AI Enterprise Globalization & ESG Innovation Consultation
As Chinese AI enterprises expand globally and pursue ESG innovation, traditional consulting cannot satisfy their urgent demand for professional advice and decision support, creating a gap that limits sustainable international growth.
1.1 Urgency
Traditional consulting is slow (3–6 months per project), expensive (hundreds of thousands to millions of RMB), and inefficient, creating barriers for SMEs and startups. Moreover, fast-changing AI technologies and global ESG regulations make traditional services unable to provide real-time insights, exposing firms to compliance risks.
1.2 Affected Stakeholders
Chinese AI enterprises: Both large firms and startups face challenges with global ESG rules and overseas market entry.
Internal teams: ESG, international business, and technical staff lack accessible professional guidance tools.
Global ESG ecosystem: Inadequate consulting slows the development of a sustainable global AI ecosystem and exposes Chinese AI firms to reputation and regulatory risks.
Academic community: Students and faculty lack up-to-date industrial insights; the platform offers timely access to cutting-edge industry knowledge.
2.Users: Target Segments & Characteristics
Four core user groups with distinct traits and needs form the platform’s target audience, all focused on AI enterprise globalization and ESG practice:
User Group	Characteristics	Core Needs
1. Chinese AI Enterprise Management Teams	Mid-senior managers (CEO/CTO/ESG Director) with 5–15 years of experience; responsible for strategic decision-making, balancing business growth and ESG compliance	Concise, data-driven insights on market entry strategies, ESG risk assessment, and competitive analysis in target international markets; efficient, actionable recommendations integrable into corporate strategy
2. ESG & International Business Teams	Specialized analysts/ international business developers with 2–8 years of experience; responsible for implementing ESG practices and executing global expansion plans	Detailed guidance on ESG reporting standards, cross-border data compliance, and local market cultural adaptation; workflow streamlining tools and real-time access to updated regulatory information
3. AI Startup Teams	Founders and core team members of seed to Series A AI startups with global ambitions; limited operational and financial resources	Affordable, easily accessible consulting services; foundational guidance on ESG compliance, global market validation, and international investor requirement alignment
4. University Faculty and Students	Industry-bound students (majoring in AI, business, ESG and related fields) and educators responsible for teaching the latest industrial knowledge and practical cases; rely on traditional, outdated information sources	A timely, cutting-edge information access channel for global AI industry trends and ESG practice cases; real-world industry data and case references for academic learning, research and teaching practice
3.Solution:
HQ (Haiwen) AI-Powered Self-Service Consultation Platform
A vertical AI platform designed for Chinese AI companies’ globalization and ESG demands (similar to Alibaba’s AQ platform but for AI/ESG). It combines a fine-tuned LLM with specialized datasets (global ESG rules, AI market trends, cross-border practices) to offer real-time, personalized consulting and decision support.
Cloud-based, scalable, cross-region accessible, and integrable with enterprise systems (CRM, ESG tools), it has two core modules:
AI Consultation Chatbot: Natural-language Q&A for ESG compliance, market entry, and cross-border AI deployment, with instant data-driven responses.
Case Study Library: Curated successful cases of Chinese AI firms’ globalization and ESG innovation, offering practical best practices.
4.Success Metrics
Five quantifiable metrics measuring adoption, value, function, viability, and documentation:
4.1 User Engagement & Adoption: 30+ registered users in 8 weeks, proving market demand.
4.2 User Validation & Satisfaction: 20+ user tests; average score ≥4.0/5.0, confirming user fit.
4.3 Functional Completion: Full end-to-end functions for both core modules.
4.4 External Support: ≥2 letters of interest from external organizations, validating commercial viability.
4.5 Documentation Quality: Professional technical docs and user manuals, supporting knowledge transfer.
5.Conclusion
The HQ platform fills a key market gap for Chinese AI enterprises expanding globally and pursuing ESG goals. As a cost-effective self-service tool, it helps them navigate global markets and achieve sustainable growth. It also acts as an academic-industry bridge linking AI/ESG research with practice.
The project matches ENT208TC’s focus on user-centered design, iterative validation, and professional documentation, giving the team real experience building market-ready AI & ESG digital products.
